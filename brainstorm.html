Brainstorming Research Topics with AI

Not sure where to start? You’re not alone. Choosing a research topic is often the hardest part—and that’s where AI can help. Tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity can help you explore ideas, expand your thinking, and narrow down a topic that’s just right for your assignment.

🔎 What You Can Do

  • Turn a broad idea (like “climate change”) into specific questions you could research.
  • Discover different angles, subfields, or case studies within your topic.
  • Find connections between your interests and academic disciplines.

🧠 Try These AI Prompts

Open a chat with your preferred AI tool and try one of these:

  • Suggest 5 possible research questions about climate justice in the U.S.
  • What are some ethical dilemmas related to artificial intelligence in education?
  • How could I explore the relationship between music and memory in a research paper?

Don’t like the answers? Ask follow-up questions like "Make these more focused" or "What would a psychology student focus on?"

🎯 Narrowing Your Focus

AI can give you a starting point—but you decide where to go from there. Once you see some topic ideas, ask yourself:

  • Which ones actually interest me?
  • Can I find enough credible sources on this?
  • Does this fit the assignment or course theme?

📚 Connect to Library Resources

Once you have a working topic, it’s time to check whether there’s research on it. Use your library's databases to search articles and books. You can even ask AI:

Help me turn this topic into search keywords for an academic database.

⚖️ Academic Integrity Reminder

AI can help you think, explore, and question—but it can’t do your thinking for you. Be sure your topic reflects your own understanding and is shaped by your own interests and goals. Don’t copy an AI-generated topic exactly without adapting or refining it.
